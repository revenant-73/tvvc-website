About TVVC

Our Story

Tualatin Valley Volleyball Club was founded in 2024 with a clear purpose: to make high-quality volleyball accessible, joyful, and rooted in what matters most—the growth of the athlete.

As a new coach at Century High School, our director recognized a gap in the local volleyball landscape. Too many families were priced out of club sports, or overwhelmed by demanding schedules and rigid training that left little room for joy. TVVC was born to change that.

We launched our first season in 2024–2025 with six teams and a commitment to do things differently:

  • Affordability – We keep costs low so more athletes can play.
  • Balance – Our time-conscious schedule minimizes travel and honors family life.
  • Joyful Learning – Practices are grounded in play, exploration, and confidence-building.

Our mission goes beyond wins and rankings. We aim to spark a lifelong love of the game, nurture curiosity, and help each player grow into a confident, adaptable human being—on and off the court.

Our Core Values

These ten principles guide everything we do at TVVC—from how we design practices to how we communicate with athletes. They reflect our commitment to developing not just better volleyball players, but confident, resilient individuals.

Curiosity Over Certainty

We embrace questions, exploration, and learning through experience—because growth comes from the unknown.

Adaptability in Action

Our training environments prepare athletes to thrive in dynamic, unpredictable game situations—not just controlled drills.

Athlete Ownership

Players are active participants in their learning journey, empowered to reflect, make choices, and find their own solutions.

Connection Before Correction

We build trust and understanding first—because meaningful change only happens in safe, supportive relationships.

Play with Purpose

Every activity is rooted in the context of the game, helping athletes connect movement with intention.

Resilience Through Challenge

Struggle isn't failure—it's feedback. We welcome it as part of the growth process.

Freedom to Explore

We remove unnecessary constraints and let athletes test, adjust, and discover what works for them.

Authenticity Always

Coaches and players bring their whole selves. We don't fake intensity or force perfection—we show up real.

Joy in the Process

We believe learning is best fueled by enjoyment, laughter, and shared excitement.

Support with Standards

We hold high expectations, but meet athletes where they are—guiding with empathy, not control.

Girls' Club Teams

Our girls' club teams compete in regional events through CEVA, providing a fun, affordable, and development-focused experience. We offer teams across multiple age groups and skill levels, ensuring every athlete has the opportunity to grow, play, and find the right fit for their journey.

Season Information

  • Season Length: December 2025 – May 2026
  • Practice Schedule: Two practices per week (2 hours each)
  • Tournaments: 6–7 tournaments per season (varies by team)
  • Travel: All events are within the CEVA region, with possible locations including Vancouver, Eugene, and Salem.

Club Fees

  • 12U-14U: $999
  • 15U-16U: $1099
  • 17U-18U: $1199

Fees include: coaching, practice facilities, tournament entries, uniforms, and administrative costs. Payment plans are available.

Note: Prices listed are estimates and may be subject to change before tryouts due to gym rental costs, tournament pricing, and uniform pricing changes.

Group Training COMING SOON

Elevate your game with our specialized small-group training sessions designed to develop smart, adaptable players. Full schedule will be available soon.

Our 90-minute training sessions are designed to feel like the game — not like a set of drills. We use small-group settings to create real, dynamic environments that help players grow in three key areas:

Attention

What players notice during play

Intention

What they aim to do

Calibration

How they adjust as play unfolds

This approach is fun, challenging, and rooted in how athletes actually learn best. We focus less on perfect technique and more on becoming smart, adaptable players.
